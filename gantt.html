Constructing Gantt Charts

Critical Path

  1. 1st step is to put in critical path
    1. Critical path:  the longest sequence of activities in a project that must be completed on time.
    2. Can be trivial (例： routine paper-work can be on a critical path), whilst expensive & imp. things exist that ≠ on critical path).
    3. Need critical path before creating ガント表
    4. Always drawn coming down in steps:
    5. 1 of reasons wherefore use steps is to show slippage:  what actually happened contra what should have happened
      1. Do not want there to be slippage on critical path ∵ anything going late on there means that every-thing else → late.
    6. However, 1st must discover critical path.
      1. Best method to obtain critical path is to use a network diagram
          1. Source:  Chris Croft Training
      2. Find critical path by adding up the times as you go across.
        1. Remember:  The critical path is the path with the longest times.
    7. If there is a task that can be overlapped with another on the critical path, then attempt to break the 1st one down a bit so that you can know when to begin the 2nd task.
      1. In so doing, you make the over-lap period as clear as possible
    8. If there are 2 critical paths (there are 2 equal longest paths in the network diagramme), then add one right below the other

Estimating Times & Adding Contingency

  1. 詳しくは、 see 企画管理
  2. Always spread the amt. of contingency time thro'out the criticla path, adding a little bit to each task, ∵ do not know where the problems will 'crop up'
  3. Add the contingency when you convert the Network Diagramme to a Gantt Chart
  4. Risks:
    1. If you have many 1-day or 1-week tasks
      1. Unlikely to happen
      2. In normal organisations, things are unlikely to hapeen in such a manner
      3. Altho' you may have s/t that just happens in 1 day, if you have several in a row, it is unlikely to take that day-by-day time-span (from experience, I agree。例えば、期末試験の為に勉強してた時に、そんなうまく行かなかった。笑汗)
    2. If your floating tasks are nearly critical, to wit, are expected to take nearly as as long as the tasks on the critical path, such cosntitutes a point of risk upon which you will want to keep your eye
      1. These tasks are ones that have very little 'float'
    3. If your plan has many short tasks at the end, s/t is likely to go wrong at some pt. in your project, & then you may be faced w/ rushing at the end, but if you already have many small/short tasks at the end, it is not going to be possible to 'crash' anything to get back on track ∵ the tasks are already v. short

'Floating' Tasks

  1. 詳しくは、 see 企画管理
  2. Next step is to add the 'floating' tasks
  3. 1st, add the vertical lines delineating the amt. of 'float'
  4. 2nd, decide whether to float a task earlier or later
    1. Doing earlier entails less risk (∵ do not have to worry about a task taking longer than thought)
    2. Doing a task later can sometimes better for cash-flow & even for cost (例：  do not need to pay for storage of furniture after purchasing it)
    3. Doing a task later can be better if there may be or is no information coming in
    4. Doing a task later can be better can be better if you do not want things sitting around
    5. ですが、 if you do plan for s/t do be later, do add a little buffer at the end in case it takes a little longer than intended
    6. You should also take into account the availability of resources (do not want to plan too many tasks at once)

Using Excel for Gantt Charts:  Top-10 Tips

  1. Use conditional formatting for colouring in the square
  2. Use weeks, rather than months or days
  3. You want to be able to see all of the Gantt Chart on the screen, so highlight all of the columns before adjusted the width in order to adjust the columns by the same amt.
  4. Turn the dates vertically or slightly diagonally
  5. Add the hours both vertically & horizontally
    1. If you like, you can put a graph of the resources at the bottom
  6. Copy a sheet with your hours, & change the hours to ¥¥ that you intend to spend
  7. If you have a task that does not req. any resources (just 待機時間), if you put in 'o' instead of '0', the cell will still be highlighted
  8. You can insert holidays, such as Christmas, when nothing happens by inserting add'l columns
    1. Should be done after the plan is done so that everything moves to the right, which is realistically what will happen with your plan's time in reality
  9. You can copy the bottom line (the total number of hours req'd) & copy it into the Gantt of Gantts; you, thereby, get a total for all of your projects
  10. Highlight (or remove the formatting) of tasks that have been completed

Using Gantt Charts for Optimum Results

  1. Gantt of Gantts
    2. Have a Gantt Chart for each project
    3. Take the total (number of hours) from the bottom of each one, copy them all onto your Gantt-of-Gantts summary
  2. 5 Common Mistakes for which to Be Wary
    1. Jumping straight to the Gantt Chart w/o making a Network Diagramme
    2. Wrong lvl. of granularity (want 'Goldilocks')
    3. Forgetting to add a contingency, spread along the Critical Path
    4. Neglecting the Gantt Chart once you have started
    5. Changing your plan more than once
